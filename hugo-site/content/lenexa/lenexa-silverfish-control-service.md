## What Silverfish Look Like

Silverfish are small insects with a teardrop body. They are silver or gray. They have long antennae. They move fast with a wiggling motion. Most are about 1/2 to 3/4 inch long.

You may confuse silverfish with firebrats. Firebrats look similar, but they like hotter spots, often near heaters. Silverfish usually stay in humid areas like bathrooms, laundry rooms, basements, crawl spaces, and attics.

## Signs of a Silverfish Infestation

Watch for these signs:

1. Repeated sightings over several nights.
2. Insects seen in more than one room.
3. Yellow stains or tiny holes on paper and fabrics.
4. Shed skins that look like thin, clear flakes.
5. Pepper-like droppings in drawers or shelves.
6. Damage to books, cardboard, wallpaper edges, or stored clothing.

## Prevention Steps That Work

You can lower risk with steady home habits:

1. Keep indoor humidity lower, especially in damp rooms.
2. Repair leaks under sinks, behind washers, and near water heaters.
3. Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and laundry spaces.
4. Seal gaps around plumbing penetrations and baseboards.
5. Reduce cardboard and loose paper stacks in storage rooms.
6. Store books, files, and fabrics in sealed containers.
7. Vacuum baseboards, closet edges, and utility corners often.
8. Keep crawl spaces and attics dry and well ventilated.

These steps support treatment and reduce repeat activity.
