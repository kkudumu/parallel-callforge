## What Are Silverfish?

Silverfish are small, wingless insects. They have a teardrop shape and move fast — a quick darting motion across your floor or wall. They're silver-gray in color and usually half an inch to one inch long. Most homeowners first spot them in bathrooms, laundry rooms, or basements.

They're not just unsettling to look at. They cause real damage.

Silverfish feed on starches found in paper, cardboard, fabrics, book bindings, and wallpaper paste. They can destroy stored documents, clothing, and insulation over time. What starts as a few insects can quietly become a widespread problem before you notice the full extent of it.

## Signs You Have a Silverfish Problem

Look for these warning signs:

- Small, pepper-like droppings near books, boxes, or stored clothes
- Yellow staining on paper or fabric
- Irregular holes chewed in wallpaper, books, or clothing
- Shed skins — silvery, papery husks left behind as they grow
- Live silverfish spotted at night near moisture sources

Silverfish are nocturnal. They hide during the day. If you're seeing them regularly, the population is likely larger than you think.

## Why Silverfish Thrive in Lenexa Homes

Kansas weather creates tough conditions for homeowners. Humidity spikes during spring and fall rains. Basements built on clay soil hold moisture. Poor drainage around foundations keeps things damp year-round.

These are exactly the conditions silverfish love.

Silverfish infestations in Lenexa tend to peak in spring and fall. As temperatures shift, humidity moves into homes and silverfish populations grow. Attics, crawl spaces, and basements become active harborage zones. That's when most homeowners start noticing the damage.

Year-round monitoring matters — but spring and fall are the highest-risk seasons. Don't wait for visible damage before calling a professional.

## Health Risks and Property Damage

Silverfish don't bite. They don't transmit disease directly. But they're not harmless.

They trigger allergies in some people. Shed skins and droppings can irritate respiratory systems. More seriously, silverfish cause significant property damage — to books, photos, stored clothing, wallpaper, and even dry goods if packaging is compromised.

The longer they stay, the more they destroy. This is not a pest to ignore.
