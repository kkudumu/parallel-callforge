## What Silverfish Look Like (Identification)
Silverfish are small, wingless insects. Most are about 1/2 to 3/4 inch long. Their bodies are flat and shaped like a teardrop. They are silver to gray and look shiny under light. They move fast and wiggle side to side, almost like a fish.

You may spot them in:
- Bathrooms
- Laundry rooms
- Basements
- Crawl spaces
- Closets
- Attics
- Behind baseboards
- Around stored boxes and books

Homes in Athens often have the exact indoor conditions silverfish like: steady warmth and moisture. They do best around 70 to 85 degrees and high humidity.

## Signs You Have a Silverfish Problem
Most people do not see silverfish during the day. They come out at night. Look for these warning signs:
- Small holes in paper, cardboard, wallpaper, or fabric
- Yellowish stains on books, papers, or clothing
- Tiny dark droppings that look like black pepper
- Shed skins in corners or behind stored items
- Live silverfish when lights turn on in damp rooms

Check near water lines, under sinks, around tubs, and in closets with poor airflow. Also check old boxes, holiday decorations, and paper files.

## Seasonal Timing in Georgia
Silverfish can be active all year in climate-controlled homes, but pressure shifts with moisture and indoor living patterns in Georgia.

- Winter (December-February): Activity stays indoors. Bathrooms, laundry rooms, and storage areas get more pressure because homes stay closed up.
- Spring (March-May): Rain and humidity rise. Damp crawl spaces and wall voids can trigger new movement.
- Summer (June-August): Heat plus humidity support faster pest activity in poorly ventilated rooms.
- Fall (September-November): Indoor activity is easier to notice as people spend more time inside and begin storage cleanups.
