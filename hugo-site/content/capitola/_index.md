## Seasonal Pest Guide for Capitola

Understanding how pest behavior shifts throughout the year helps you stay prepared and proactive. Our local team adjusts treatment protocols based on these predictable seasonal patterns.

### Spring and Summer: The Ant Invasion

Spring brings a massive surge in insect reproductive activity. As the beautiful flowers bloom around the area and temperatures rise, ants, wasps, and other stinging insects become highly active. Summer requires vigilant, ongoing perimeter defense to stop ants from aggressively invading kitchens and bathrooms in their relentless search for moisture and food crumbs. We also see increased wasp nesting activity under eaves and around deck structures during the warmer months, requiring safe, professional removal.

### Fall and Winter: Rodents on the Move

The cooler, wetter months dramatically shift our primary focus from insects to rodents. Roof rats, Norway rats, and house mice aggressively seek shelter from the harsh coastal rains. We strongly recommend that homeowners check their weather stripping, inspect roof vents, and ensure all crawlspace screens are completely intact before the first heavy rain hits the coast. Proactive winterization of your home is the most effective defense against seasonal rodent infestations.
