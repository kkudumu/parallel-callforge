## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if I have a silverfish problem or just occasional sightings?

One silverfish every so often can happen, but repeated sightings in the same rooms, especially at night, usually point to a persistent source. If you keep seeing them in bathrooms, closets, laundry areas, or near stored paper items, schedule an inspection so the source can be identified.

### What does a silverfish control visit typically include?

Most visits include an inspection of active areas, identification of likely moisture or harborage conditions, and a treatment recommendation based on layout and activity level. You should also receive prep and post-treatment guidance so monitoring is clear after service.

### How quickly can treatment start after I call?

Scheduling depends on provider availability, route load, and the scope needed for your home. When you call, ask for the earliest inspection window, what happens at that first visit, and whether treatment can begin immediately after findings are reviewed.

### Are treatment options available for homes with kids or pets?

Many providers can discuss treatment approaches and placement strategies that account for children and pets. Ask what products or methods are being considered, where they are applied, how long access should be limited, and what safety steps you should follow.

### Why do silverfish keep coming back in humid areas?

Silverfish persist where moisture and shelter remain available. If underlying conditions like damp zones, ventilation issues, or cluttered storage are not addressed, activity can return even after initial treatment. A plan that combines treatment with moisture and harborage reduction is typically more durable.

### What should I do before and after service?

Before service, follow provider instructions on access, storage movement, and any cleaning limits. After service, follow re-entry and cleaning guidance exactly, monitor known activity spots, and report any continued sightings so the provider can adjust next steps if needed.

### How do I verify a contractor’s license and insurance in Florida?

Ask the contractor for current license and insurance details in writing, then verify that information through the appropriate Florida licensing and business records channels. Keep copies of the scope of work and documentation before authorizing service.
