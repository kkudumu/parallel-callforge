## Why Silverfish Need Fast Action
Silverfish are quiet pests. They hide in dark and damp spots. They come out at night. They feed on starches and cellulose. That means paper, glue, fabric, and cardboard are food sources.

In homes in Deland, silverfish stay active all year because heat and humidity stay high for long periods. Even air-conditioned homes can have damp pockets behind walls, under sinks, around water heaters, and inside closets.

A small issue can spread. Silverfish live a long time. They keep laying eggs in hidden spaces. By the time you notice one room, activity may already be in several spots.

## Identification Guide
Silverfish are easy to miss, but you can learn what to look for.

What they look like:
- About 1/2 inch long
- Silver-gray color
- Teardrop-shaped body
- Long antennae
- Fast, wiggling movement

Where they hide:
- Bathrooms and laundry rooms
- Under sinks and around plumbing lines
- Closets with stored boxes
- Attics and garages
- Behind baseboards and wallpaper seams

What they damage:
- Books and magazines
- Photo albums and documents
- Wallpaper paste
- Natural-fiber clothes
- Cardboard storage boxes

## Signs of an Infestation
You may not see many live insects at first. Most homeowners notice clues before they notice the pest.

Common signs include:
- Tiny holes in paper and fabric
- Yellow stains or scrape marks on books
- Ragged edges on stored documents
- Small black droppings that look like pepper
- Shed skins in corners and storage areas

## FAQ
### How do I know if I have a silverfish problem?
Look for small silver-gray insects in damp areas, plus tiny holes in paper, glue damage on book bindings, and droppings that look like dark pepper. Most activity appears at night.

### What damage can silverfish cause?
They can damage photos, books, wallpaper, documents, clothing, and boxes. Items with starches, glue, or natural fibers are most at risk.

### Are silverfish dangerous to people?
They are not known for biting, but their shed skins and droppings may irritate allergy-sensitive people. The bigger issue is damage to stored belongings.

### Are treatments safe for children and pets?
Licensed technicians can explain treatment choices and safety steps before service begins. Always share your household needs so the plan fits your home.

### How can I prevent silverfish after treatment?
Lower humidity, fix leaks, improve ventilation, seal small gaps, and store important items in sealed plastic bins instead of cardboard.

### How fast should I act?
Act as soon as you see signs. Early treatment is usually simpler and less costly than waiting for spread.
