## Common Pest Problems in Santa Cruz

The mild coastal weather keeps pest activity steady through much of the year. Unlike inland areas that see harder seasonal slowdowns, properties in Santa Cruz often need ongoing prevention because moisture and moderate temperatures support nesting, foraging, and indoor movement.

### Ants, Spiders, and Moisture-Driven Intruders

Odorous house ants are one of the most common calls we receive, especially in kitchens, bathrooms, and around foundation cracks. Moisture near windows, under sinks, and in crawl spaces gives ants easy access. Spiders also thrive in garages, sheds, eaves, and quiet storage areas, particularly in neighborhoods near greenbelts or homes tucked closer to wooded sections near UCSC.

Coastal dampness can also attract earwigs, silverfish, and cockroaches. These pests are often linked to leaking pipes, dense landscaping, cluttered storage, and poor ventilation. In older homes around the Eastside or near Downtown, small gaps in foundations and siding can make recurring infestations more likely.

### Rodents in Attics, Crawl Spaces, and Wall Voids

Rats and mice are a major concern during cooler and wetter periods. As temperatures drop and rain picks up, rodents look for dry shelter and reliable food sources. Attics, basements, detached garages, and crawl spaces are all common entry points. Roofline gaps, damaged vents, and worn weatherstripping are frequent problem areas, especially in coastal homes exposed to wind and moisture.

Rodents are not just a nuisance. They can chew wiring, contaminate insulation, and spread bacteria through droppings and urine. A complete rodent service should include inspection, exclusion, trapping, and sanitation guidance so the issue does not return a few weeks later.

### Termites and Wood-Destroying Pests

Termites are a serious issue in Santa Cruz because many homes have exposed wood features, aging decks, fencing, and trim that stay damp longer due to fog and marine air. Subterranean termites are often tied to soil contact and moisture around the foundation, while drywood termites can move into framing, fascia boards, and attic wood.

If you have seen discarded wings, mud tubes, hollow-sounding wood, or bubbling paint, it is smart to schedule an inspection quickly. Early termite treatment can help prevent structural repairs that become much more expensive over time.

## Seasonal Pest Activity on the Central Coast

Pest control here works best when treatment follows the seasons, even though activity never fully stops.

### January and February: Rodent and Roach Prevention

During the colder, wetter part of winter, mice, rats, and cockroaches are common indoor invaders. Rain drives pests out of exterior harborage and into wall voids, kitchens, utility rooms, and crawl spaces. This is the right time for sealing entry points, improving sanitation, and addressing moisture issues before populations grow.

### March: Ants, Termites, and Spiders Start Ramping Up

As conditions begin to dry out, ant trails become more visible, termite swarmers may appear, and spider activity increases around eaves, garages, and outdoor storage. Preventive treatment in early spring helps limit nesting and reduces the chance of larger infestations heading into warmer months.
